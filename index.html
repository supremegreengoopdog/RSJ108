How to Make a Short Film ...

Time: 2 weeks to several months
Helpfullness: 100% Helpful

Have you ever wanted to make a short film? It seems like a really hard process and you might not know where to start, but following these simple steps can help you make the short film of your dreams! Whether you have equipment that all the pros use or just a camera, there's always a place to start!

  • Things You'll Need

  • Camera/Lighting Equipment (can range from professional equipment or something as simple as a phone!)
  • Crew
  • Actors
  • Video Editing Software

  1. Brainstorm Some Ideas!

    Before starting anything, take out a paper and start noting down any ideas you get. It doesn't matter whether they're good or bad, just write down anything! After you come up with several ideas, pick out your favorite one and go along with it!

  2. Write a Script!

    Now that you have your idea, it's time to start writing a script! Start with a rough draft that details all your scenes with what your characters will do, and if possible then ask friends to review it! Give the final touches and you have your official script!

  3. Scout For Locations!

    Look for any locations that'd fit as the scene for your short film! Remember: if you want to film in an owned property, make sure you first ask for consent to film in that location!

  4. Get Some Actors!

    Your short film can't be made without some actors to play your characters! It's always easiest to ask friends and family to play your characters, or if you'd like you can instead seek professional actors!

  5. Get a Crew!

    Just like actors, your crew is essential for your film! If you plan on having more equipment than just a camera, you can also ask your friends and family to be responsible for using that equipment during the shooting! Or, like actors, you can seek for others who are more experienced!

  6. Rehearse the Script with Actors!

    Your actors definitely need to be familiar with the script to play their roles properly! You can rehearse the script with them before filming to get them warmed up to their characters! Either simply just reading out the script or acting it both work out!

  8. Time to Film!

    You're now officially ready to record your short film! Go out to the locations you scouted and set up the scene with your actors and crew, then get to filming! This part might take several attempts, but take as much time as you need to get the best shots you can!

  9. Edit Your Footage!

    Now that you have all of your footage, import them to any video editing software you prefer and begin assembling the full timeline of your short film! Always remember that while editing may seem confusing, you can look up any question you have! Some useful things to consider when editing is the tone of color for scenes, as well as audio.

  10. Review Your Work!

    After your short film is fully edited, go ahead and give it a watch! Look over the contents closely and make sure it's exactly to your likings! You can also show your work to others and ask for their input, it's always important to get opinions from others in case they can catch something you didn't!
